Bunnies and hares are both members of the Leporidae family, but they are quite different in behavior and appearance. Bunnies, often referred to as rabbits, are typically smaller, have shorter ears, and live in burrows or warrens. They are social creatures that prefer to live in groups and are commonly domesticated as pets or found in controlled environments.

Hares, on the other hand, are usually larger with longer ears and legs. They are born fully furred with open eyes and are ready to move shortly after birth, unlike bunnies who are born blind and hairless. Hares live in nests above the ground and are generally more solitary. Their long legs make them well-adapted to fast running, which is crucial for escaping predators in open fields.
